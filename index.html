I’m a postdoctoral researcher in the Fairness, Accountability, Transparency, and Ethics in AI (FATE) Group at Microsoft Research Montréal. A common theme in my research is protecting user rights and well-being. I design ethically and socially responsible information and social computing systems and study how they interact with and influence their users.


My background is in information retrieval, information extraction, and data mining. I completed my PhD summa cum laude at the Max Planck Institute for Informatics and Saarland University advised by Gerhard Weikum and Krishna P. Gummadi. During that time, I was also a member of the Max Planck Institute for Software Systems. My doctoral work focused on the issues of privacy and fairness in search systems. Outside of academia, I worked as an engineering intern in the privacy infrastructure team at Google and as a software developer in e-commerce.
